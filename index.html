Enter the Manor

A Bleak Place

When Mary Lennox was sent to Misselthwaite Manor, she found a house standing on the edge of a moor. It was a big, bleak place with a hundred rooms that were shut up.

The wind wuthered round the corners of the house and whistled in the chimneys. There was no garden. She stared out of the window at the sweeping vastness of the land, wild and desolate.

The Cry In The Corridor

She heard it again—the crying sound. But then the robin came. The little brown bird who became her only friend. He hopped onto the wall and looked at her with his bright eye.

"She held the key in her hand. It was a golden key."

Magic

Magic! The sun shone down for nearly a week on the secret garden. The roses were falling, falling like rain. The garden was full of birds and flowers and sunshine.

Colin stood up. He threw his head back and laughed. He was strong. He was alive.

"The Magic is in this garden!"

"If you look the right way, you can see that the whole world is a garden."
